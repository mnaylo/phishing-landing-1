Had you been interacting with an actual phishing email, cybercriminals could have obtained important user credentials and later used them to log into other company computer systems without our knowledge. In the future, look out for the ”Red Flags” explained below.

Red Flag: The senders email address has a mis-spelling. The email was not from someone@dropbox.com.

Red Flag: The email is telling the user to click a link, hovering over the View or download link shows it will not go to Dropbox.

Red Flag: Why did you receive this email? Were you expecting to receive such an email from a known business contact?
